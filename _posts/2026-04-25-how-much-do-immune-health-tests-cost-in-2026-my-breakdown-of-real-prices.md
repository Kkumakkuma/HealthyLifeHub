## Understanding Immune Health Tests

### What Are Immune Health Tests?

Immune health tests are tools that help assess your immune system's function and identify potential issues. These tests can range from simple blood tests to advanced genetic assessments. In my experience, understanding what tests are available is crucial to making informed choices.

### Why You Might Need One

There are several reasons you might consider getting an immune health test:
- Chronic fatigue
- Frequent infections
- Autoimmune conditions
- Unexplained allergic reactions

In my case, I was suffering from recurrent colds, which prompted me to take this step.

## Types of Immune Health Tests 

### Basic Immune Function Tests

1. **CBC (Complete Blood Count)**: This standard blood test evaluates various components of the blood, including white blood cells, which are crucial for immune response. Prices typically range from $50 to $150.
  
2. **Immunoglobulin Levels**: These tests measure the levels of certain antibodies in your blood, costing between $100 to $250. 

### Advanced Testing Options

1. **Genetic Testing**: Companies like 23andMe offer tests that analyze specific genetic markers related to immune function. Prices can start around $199 for basic ancestry kits, whereas more comprehensive health insights could range from $249 to $499.

2. **Functional Immunology Tests**: Brands like Everlywell and Quest Diagnostics provide at-home tests that can evaluate various immune markers. For instance, Everlywell's "Immune Health Test" retails for approximately $159.

## Factors Influencing Costs

### Insurance Coverage

One of the most significant influences on your out-of-pocket costs is whether your insurance covers these tests. It's essential to check with your provider before scheduling any tests.

### Additional Costs

- **Consultation Fees**: If you need to consult with a specialist, expect to pay anywhere from $100 to $300, which could add to your total cost.
- **Follow-Up Tests**: If initial results indicate an issue, you might need additional tests that can further inflate expenses.
