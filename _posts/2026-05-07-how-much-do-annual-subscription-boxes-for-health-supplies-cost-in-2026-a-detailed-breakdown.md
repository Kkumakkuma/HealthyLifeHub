## Hidden Costs and Fees: What to Watch For

As I dived deeper into the subscription box world, I discovered that low upfront prices often disguise additional costs that can quickly add up. Knowing what to look for allowed me to make more informed decisions.

### Shipping and Handling

Many subscription boxes, including The Good Stuff, advertise a low monthly fee. However, I was shocked to see additional shipping and handling costs on my first invoice. The Good Stuff charges a shipping fee of about $8 per box, which significantly increases the overall cost if you're not careful. Thus, the true value of a subscription might not be evident until the final bill arrives.

### Cancellation Fees and Policies

Another point that caught me off guard was the cancellation policies. I encountered brands with 30-day notice requirements to cancel subscriptions. For example, Bulu Box includes a clause stating that if you cancel after the monthly billing cycle starts, you will still be billed for the next box. This left me with an uneasiness about managing my subscription, as I suddenly felt locked in despite any dissatisfaction.

### Discounts and Promotions

While researching, I stumbled upon promo codes and discounts for first-time subscribers, which can be alluring. However, in some cases—like with Thrive Market—the promotional price only applies to the first year, effectively doubling the membership cost in the subsequent renewal year. It was a real eye-opener for me and something to keep in mind when weighing your options.
