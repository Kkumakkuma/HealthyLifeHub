## How Does Collagen Work for Joint Health?

Collagen plays an essential role in providing structure to your joints, tendons, and cartilage, which is vital for overall joint function. The body produces collagen naturally, but as we age, this production decreases. 

### The Role of Collagen in Joint Lubrication

Collagen significantly contributes to joint lubrication. Research shows that collagen supplementation can enhance synovial fluid— the liquid found in joint cavities— allowing for smoother joint movements. 

### Enhancing Cartilage Repair

Studies published by the journal "Current Medical Research and Opinion" suggest that collagen peptides can stimulate cartilage cells to produce more collagen. This effect is thought to be beneficial for recovering from joint injuries and can potentially slow down the degeneration associated with conditions like osteoarthritis.
