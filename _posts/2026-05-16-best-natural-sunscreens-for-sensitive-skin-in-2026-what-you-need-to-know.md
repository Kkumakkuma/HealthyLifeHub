## How Does Sunscreen Work?

Sunscreens protect your skin by either absorbing or reflecting UV radiation. Chemical sunscreens use organic compounds to absorb UV rays and transform them into heat, which is released from the skin. Meanwhile, physical (or mineral) sunscreens contain active mineral ingredients, usually zinc oxide or titanium dioxide, that physically block and reflect UV radiation.

Here’s a quick breakdown of how they function:

- **Chemical Sunscreens:** Absorb UV radiation, converting it to heat.
- **Physical Sunscreens:** Reflect UV radiation, preventing it from penetrating the skin.

For sensitive skin, the better option is often physical sunscreens, as they tend to cause fewer reactions and provide broad-spectrum protection.

## What's the Difference Between Chemical and Mineral Sunscreens?

Understanding the difference between chemical and mineral sunscreens is crucial, especially for those with sensitive skin. Here’s a quick comparison:

| Feature               | Chemical Sunscreens                                   | Mineral Sunscreens                                   |
|-----------------------|------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Ingredients           | Contains organic compounds (e.g., oxybenzone, avobenzone) | Contains zinc oxide or titanium dioxide              |
| Mechanism of Action   | Absorbs UV rays, converts to heat                    | Reflects UV rays, preventing skin penetration         |
| Skin Type Suitability | May irritate sensitive skin                            | Generally safer for sensitive skin                   |
| Speed of Absorption   | Absorbs quickly into the skin                        | Sits on the skin; may leave a white cast            |
| SPF Effectiveness     | Can offer high SPF, but varies by formulation       | Effective, but generally requires reapplication more frequently |

In my experience, mineral sunscreens have been far more beneficial for my sensitive skin, providing lasting protection without irritation.
