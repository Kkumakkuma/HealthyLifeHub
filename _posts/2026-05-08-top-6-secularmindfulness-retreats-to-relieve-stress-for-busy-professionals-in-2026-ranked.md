## What Most Guides Get Wrong

1. **Not Considering Location:** 
   Many guides recommend retreats without considering their geographical nuances. Why this matters: Choosing a location that resonates with you can significantly impact your comfort and openness to the experience. For instance, I found the quiet of Vermont drastically different from the vibrance of Bali, each influencing my state of mind in unique ways.

2. **Overlooking Post-Retreat Support:** 
   Many retreats end without offering continued support or a follow-up. Why this matters: Stress management is a continuous journey; a lack of support can lead to a quick return to old habits. After my retreat in Colorado, they offered a follow-up session via Zoom, which made a huge difference in maintaining my progress.

3. **Assuming One Size Fits All:** 
   "Mindfulness can work for anyone," is a common refrain, but not everyone's needs are the same. Why this matters: Tailoring your retreat experience can maximize effectiveness. I needed emotional resilience tools, while some participants sought more spiritual connections. Choosing a retreat that aligns with your personal objectives is crucial.
